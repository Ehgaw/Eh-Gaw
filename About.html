History of Yangon

Discover how Myanmar's largest city developed over time.

Founding

Yangon was founded by the Mon people during the 11th century. It was originally known as Dagon, a small fishing village centred around the famous Shwedagon Pagoda.

British Colonisation

During the First Anglo-Burmese War and later conflicts, the British gradually gained control of Lower Burma. In 1852, Yangon became part of British Burma and was transformed into an important port and commercial centre.

World War 2 and Modernisation

Before World War 2 started, Yangon became the most important and richest city in colonial South East Asia. When Japan invaded, Yangon became ravaged and became economically underdeveloped compared to cities in Thailand or other South East Asian country. Modern day now, it has become the largest and economic center of Myanmar.
